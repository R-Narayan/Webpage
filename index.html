EVENTS




RoboWars

>ROBOWARS, the flagship event of graVITas is a one it’s kind combat robotics competition, and also one of the biggest in India and the biggest in South India. The exciting battles between the bots took place round the clock with more than 40 bots competing, in the largest and safest arena in India, to win the title and prize pool of 2.5 lakhs. Teams from around the globe competed in the weight categories of 15 kg, 30 kg and 60 kg and experienced the mayhem inside the arena. Apart from the clashing of the bots, amazing performances from the VIT Dance and the Music club enchanted the already awestruck audience.

Hands on Robotics

Hands on Robotics, was attended by more than 150 participants in which they were taught how to build mobile robots including laptop controlled robot, line follower robot and obstacle avoider robot using IR and ultrasonic sensors, gesture controlled robot using accelerometer. At the end of the workshop, an intense and exciting competition between the participants was held where they had to build line follower robots and traverse a given maze in minimum time.

Machine Design

Machine Design with Fusion 360 is a yearly workshop organized by RoboVitics in which the participants are taught how to use the Fusion 360 Software. The participants are exposed to the basics of designing, 2D sketches and drawing, 3D modelling and new features of the software like “Extrude” and “Revolve”. For this year’s workshop, participants were taught the assembly of the radial engine. After this, Motion Study Radial Engine was done. It was followed by Rendering which adds aesthetics to the designed 3D models. Simulation was also conducted which tells about how a particular object will react if its put under some certain stress or some particular condition. Lastly, they were taught about Generative Designing which, a new feature in the design industry.

PROJECTS

The Self Driving Car

The Self Driving Car project is an Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning based project which aims at the automation of cars where the cars learn to drive by analysing the user and it will then drive on its own without any intervention.

Connect 4

Connect-4_expert is an artificially intelligent system based on the connect 4 board game. It can be played as single player with the AI as well as multiplayer. The AI uses mini-max algorithm with alpha-beta pruning to predict moves. The game can be played from anywhere, as it uses socket programming for connecting the human player(client) and the AI(server)

The Cozmo Bot

The cozmo bot is a prototype in quest to make a friendly robot. The bot uses AI and Image processing to detect and track human faces and has the ability to recognize human speech and act upon it. A Raspberry Pi controls various servos and other motors via an Arduino UNO for actuating gestures such as performing handshake and neck movements.

Cheetah

The cheetah is a quadruped bot based on the animal cheetah. The bot has a 3d printed body and is made of two pieces connected by a spine which is a novel approach for a quadruped as all current quadrupeds have a single piece body. It is capable of following a person and going to designated places using a camera connected to a raspberry pi. It uses servos as actuators to make it walk and move its head as required.

Writing Machine

This project is based on Core XY axis writing machine which is formulated to write with pen. It not only writes any sort of document but also can sketch according to the Gcode given. . The mechanism was set and carried out with the electrical components which includes Arduino and CNC shield. Later, the Gcode was generated with the help of benbox and was processed on the system for output.

Robotic arm

it detects colours to place boxes to the designated place.

Vr based bot

the bot connects to an app using raspberry pi and camera and displays what is being seen in a vr. Bot is capable of going to remote places and send imagery directly to a mobile.

Door lock

a futuristic door locking system that requires not only username and password but also uses facial and fingerprint recognition all built into a local server

3d mapping drone

the drone has an ion board camera that takes images and transmits them to a cloud to form a 3d terrain of any area

Self-assembling cubes

The mechanism uses a flywheel built into 3d printed cube. The flywheel is present on 3 sides of the cube making it capable of flipping in any direction. A number of these cubes can be controlled to form a larger cube or body of desired shape.

OUTREACH

Kartavya

As an initiative by Robovitics, we have a vision to spread our reach outside the gates of our institution. We decided to undertake a program to meet and interact with underprivileged kids from various schools and orphanages in Tamil Nadu and beyond. Every year we select a group of aspiring students from our club who are interested and capable of spreading the knowledge of robotics among young minds and making them envision the heights of innovation that can be achieved in robotics. We donate not only monetarily but also them with basic electrical and electronic components to help them know this subject better. Last year, a group of Club Volunteers went to interact with children in a few local schools in Vellore. We held robotics workshops and an exhibition about our club’s projects and ideas. We extended our outreach to a school in Mumbai. Our club has been ties with Velammal Educational Trust schools in Chennai and Vellore for the past three to four years, where we visit schools and interact with students. As proud RoboVITians we aspire to not only gain knowledge but spread it to the best of our capabities.

ABOUT US

We are a group of aspiring engineers with an interest in the field of robotics. What started out as a couple of nobodies over a decade ago with an inclination to do something new, today we have grown not only in terms of size but also intellect. RoboVITics comprises of students from various batches across multiple disciplines. We aim to spread the knowledge of Robotics which has been developed through constant motivation, that led to the development of a big community enlightening young minds. We work on various projects that leave the beholders in awe. RoboVITics also incubates various teams that specialise in different fields of robotics, they participate in a wide range of national and international competitions providing the club with the necessary outreach into the community. Team Orcus is the combat robotics team of RoboVITics, representing our club and by extension our prestigious institution in various combat robotics events across the country. All our RoboVITians strive to live up to the legacy set by our predecessors, with pride and affection towards the club.
